*Last updated: 2026-03-18*

## 1. Competitor Profiles

### 1.1 Trivia Crack (Preguntados) -- Active, dominant

**Developer:** Etermax (Argentina)
**Status:** Active, regularly updated (latest: v3.351, Jan 2026)
**User base:** 600M+ downloads, ~150M annual active users, ~330K downloads/month

**Core features:**
- Turn-based 1v1 multiplayer trivia (6 categories, spin-the-wheel mechanic)
- Multiple choice questions only
- Live mode and classic (async) mode
- User-generated content via Trivia Crack Creators Program
- Pet system, academy, collectibles (gamification-heavy)
- Available in 30+ languages

**Monetization:** ~$36M annual revenue
- Ads (~75% of revenue, ~500M impressions/day)
- In-app purchases (gems, coins, extra lives)
- Premium version ($9.99 one-time or $4.99/mo subscription for ad-free)

**Voice features:** None

**Strengths:**
- Massive network effects -- easy to find opponents
- Strong in Latin America and Spanish-speaking markets
- Proven freemium-to-ads flywheel
- Mature product with years of iteration

**Weaknesses:**
- Ad-heavy free experience degrades gameplay
- No innovation in question format (MCQ only, no free-form)
- No voice interaction, no hands-free mode
- Gamification bloat (currencies, pets, tickets) can overwhelm new users

### 1.2 Kahoot! -- Active, education-focused

**Developer:** Kahoot! ASA (Norway)
**Status:** Active, publicly traded company
**User base:** 11B+ cumulative participants (non-unique), 30M+ public quizzes

**Core features:**
- Live multiplayer quizzes (host + participants model)
- AI question generator
- Multiple question types (MCQ, puzzles, polls, open-ended text, slides)
- Offline/test mode for self-study
- Content marketplace for creators
- Audio, video, and image embedding in questions
- Reporting and analytics

**Monetization:** SaaS subscription model
- Free tier (basic hosting, limited features)
- Starter: $10/mo
- 360 plans: $20-$49/mo
- Enterprise pricing for organizations
- Marketplace revenue share with content creators

**Voice features:** Limited -- supports screen readers (VoiceOver, NVDA) and dictation via third-party tools (Dragon NaturallySpeaking). No native voice interaction.

**Strengths:**
- Dominant in education and corporate training
- Strong brand recognition
- AI-powered question generation
- Open-ended question support (text-based, not voice)
- Robust analytics

**Weaknesses:**
- Designed for group/classroom settings, not solo play
- Requires a host to run sessions (not self-service trivia)
- No hands-free or driving mode
- No CarPlay integration
- Subscription pricing targets organizations, not casual users

### 1.3 QuizUp -- Defunct (shut down March 2021)

**Developer:** Plain Vanilla Games (Iceland), later acquired by Glu Mobile
**Status:** Shut down March 22, 2021
**User base:** 100M users at peak, 1B+ matches in first 6 months

**Core features:**
- Real-time 1v1 multiplayer (simultaneous play, not turn-based)
- 1,200+ topic categories
- Social features (profiles, friend challenges, topic communities)
- User-submitted questions
- MCQ format, timed answers

**Monetization:** Struggled critically with monetization
- Acquired by Glu Mobile for $7.5M (fire sale price)
- Attempted ads and in-app purchases post-acquisition -- users left rather than paid
- "Users were passionate, not profitable"

**Voice features:** None

**Strengths (historical):**
- Best-in-class real-time multiplayer matching
- Deep topic specialization (1,200+ categories)
- Strong community/social features

**Weaknesses / Lessons learned:**
- Never found a sustainable revenue model
- Users rejected monetization attempts
- Glu Mobile neglected the product for 5 years post-acquisition
- Demonstrated that engagement alone does not equal revenue

**Relevance to Hangs:** QuizUp proved massive demand for trivia but also proved that "free trivia with MCQ" is a commodity. Differentiation matters. Their failure to monetize is a cautionary tale -- Hangs's AI evaluation is a potential value-add worth paying for.

### 1.4 HQ Trivia -- Defunct (shut down February 2020)

**Developer:** Intermedia Labs (USA)
**Status:** Shut down February 14, 2020
**User base:** 2.3M concurrent users at peak (March 2018), valued at $100M

**Core features:**
- Live scheduled game shows (appointment-based, not on-demand)
- Real cash prizes split among winners
- Host-led video format (like a TV game show)
- MCQ, 10 seconds per question, progressive difficulty
- Brand partnerships (Nike, Wendy's, Warner Bros.)

**Monetization:**
- Venture-funded ($15M from Founders Fund)
- Sponsored games from brands
- Never achieved self-sustaining revenue

**Voice features:** None (passive watching + tapping)

**Strengths (historical):**
- Created genuine cultural moments (appointment TV for mobile)
- Proved live trivia has mass appeal
- Innovative format at launch

**Weaknesses / Lessons learned:**
- Appointment-based model killed retention -- users lost interest when they missed shows
- Technical instability at scale (glitches, crashes)
- Overextended into line extensions (HQ Words, HQ Sports) instead of perfecting core
- Internal dysfunction (co-founder death, exec clashes)
- Users who lost early had nothing to do -- no engagement loop

**Relevance to Hangs:** HQ proved trivia can be a cultural moment but appointment-based models are fragile. Hangs's on-demand, play-anytime model avoids this trap entirely.

### 1.5 Jeopardy! World Tour -- Active, brand-driven

**Developer:** Sony Pictures Television / Uken Games
**Status:** Active on iOS and Android; also on Apple Arcade (World Tour+)
**User base:** ~20K downloads/month, ~$100K monthly revenue

**Core features:**
- Classic Jeopardy! format (categories, Daily Double, Final Jeopardy)
- 25,000+ categories
- 3-player matches against online opponents
- World travel progression mechanic (unlock cities)
- Tournaments and events
- Offline mode
- Power-ups for competitive advantage

**Monetization:**
- Free-to-play with in-app purchases ($1.99-$6.99)
- In-game currency (cash) required for entry
- Watch ads to earn currency
- Apple Arcade version (included in subscription)

**Voice features:** The Jeopardy! brand has a partnership with Drivetime/Drive.fm for voice-activated hands-free play in the car. The main app itself does not have native voice controls.

**Strengths:**
- Iconic brand recognition
- Offline mode
- Proven format that people already understand
- Apple Arcade distribution

**Weaknesses:**
- Standard MCQ (not the open-ended answer format of real Jeopardy!)
- In-app purchase pressure (currency gating)
- Relatively small user base for such a big brand
- Voice play only via third-party Drivetime integration, not native

### 1.6 Trivia Royale -- Defunct (shut down September 2023)

**Developer:** Teatime Games (Iceland) -- same founder as QuizUp (Thor Fridriksson)
**Status:** Shut down September 2023
**User base:** 3M+ downloads in first month, 30M+ total

**Core features:**
- Battle royale format (1,024 players, single elimination)
- Head-to-head rounds (5 questions each)
- "Game Face" avatar creation using face-tracking camera
- 100,000+ questions
- Royale Lounge social area for winners

**Monetization:** Failed to monetize
- Struggled with same problem as QuizUp
- Acquisition talks with a "big tech" company fell through
- Ran out of runway

**Voice features:** None

**Strengths (historical):**
- Innovative battle royale format for trivia
- Viral growth (2M downloads in 2 weeks)
- Fun avatar system

**Weaknesses / Lessons learned:**
- Same founder, same monetization failure as QuizUp
- Battle royale format means most players lose quickly (poor retention)
- Large-scale multiplayer requires large concurrent player base
- Proved again: viral trivia downloads =/= revenue

**Relevance to Hangs:** Trivia Royale is a second data point (after QuizUp) that MCQ trivia with multiplayer and virality alone cannot sustain a business. The market is littered with trivia apps that got downloads but not revenue.

**Developer:** Drivetime (USA)
**Status:** Active (rebranded to Drive.fm)
**Core concept:** Voice-first trivia and audio games designed for driving

**Core features:**

**Monetization:** Freemium + subscription ($10/mo for all content)

**Strengths:**
- Proven voice-first trivia works in the car

- 2020 Webby Award for Best Voice App

**Weaknesses:**
- Content-based (pre-recorded episodes), not AI-generated
- No AI evaluation -- answers are matched against expected responses

- No image questions
- Subscription model ($10/mo) for full access is steep for casual users
- Limited to English

## 3. Positioning Statement

**Hangs is the only trivia app where you speak your answers in your own words and an AI evaluates whether you are right.**

Every other trivia app is multiple-choice: you pick A, B, C, or D. Hangs lets you actually *know* the answer, not just recognize it from a list. This is fundamentally harder, more satisfying, and more educational.

Combined with hands-free voice control, Hangs is built for moments when your eyes and hands are busy -- driving, cooking, exercising -- but your brain is free. It turns dead time into a trivia challenge without requiring you to look at or touch your phone.

**One-liner:** "Trivia you actually have to know, not just guess. Completely hands-free."

## 4. Market Gap Analysis

### Gaps Hangs fills that no competitor addresses:

**Gap 1: AI-evaluated free-form answers**
Every major trivia app uses multiple choice. Kahoot! has text-entry open-ended questions but only for hosted classroom settings, not solo play, and uses exact string matching rather than AI evaluation. Hangs's GPT-powered evaluation accepts natural language answers, handles partial correctness, and provides explanations. This is a genuine technical moat -- it requires AI infrastructure that casual game studios do not have.

**Gap 2: Voice-first with visual richness**
Drivetime is voice-first but audio-only (no images, no visual questions). Kahoot and Trivia Crack have images but no voice interaction. Hangs bridges both: image questions (silhouettes, blind maps, hint images) with voice answers. When you can look at the screen (passenger, at home), you get richer content. When you cannot (driving), it works purely by voice.

**Gap 3: AI-generated, infinitely scalable question pool**
Most competitors rely on human-curated question databases (expensive to maintain, finite). Hangs's question generation pipeline (GPT-4 + self-critique) can produce unlimited questions on any topic, with quality scoring built in. Trivia Crack relies on user submissions. Kahoot's AI generation is for hosts creating quizzes, not for the app's own content.

**Gap 4: Multi-language with AI evaluation**
Trivia Crack supports many languages but only for MCQ (translations of fixed answers). Hangs's AI evaluation works across languages -- a Czech speaker can answer in Czech, and GPT evaluates correctness in that language. This is not a feature any competitor offers.

**Gap 5: No CarPlay trivia app exists**
Despite millions of daily commuters, there is no trivia app on CarPlay. Drivetime works as a regular app while driving but has no CarPlay integration. This is an unclaimed distribution channel.

## 5. Threats

### What competitors could do to close the gap:

**Threat 1: Trivia Crack adds voice mode (Medium risk)**
Etermax has the user base and resources. But adding voice-first interaction requires rearchitecting their entire UX, and their ad-driven model conflicts with hands-free play (how do you show banner ads to someone who is not looking at the screen?). Their incentive to cannibalize their own ad revenue is low.

**Threat 2: Kahoot! moves into consumer solo play (Medium risk)**
Kahoot already has open-ended questions and AI generation. If they pivot from classroom-first to consumer-first with a solo voice mode, they would be a formidable competitor. However, their entire business model and brand is built around hosted group experiences. A solo voice trivia mode would be a significant strategic departure.

**Threat 3: Apple or Google builds it into their assistant (High risk, low probability)**
Siri and Google Assistant could theoretically run trivia with voice interaction. Apple has added more interactive Siri features. If Apple shipped "Hey Siri, quiz me" with AI evaluation, it would be hard to compete with a built-in feature. However, platform companies historically do not build deep vertical experiences like this.

**Threat 4: Drivetime adds AI evaluation (High risk)**
Drivetime is the most direct competitor. They already have voice-first driving trivia. If they add GPT-powered free-form evaluation, they would match Hangs's core differentiator. Mitigating factor: Drivetime is a content/media company (pre-recorded episodes, Jeopardy! license), not an AI company. Adding real-time AI evaluation would change their entire architecture and cost structure.

**Threat 5: A new entrant from the AI space (Medium risk)**
An AI-native startup (or even OpenAI themselves via ChatGPT voice mode) could build voice trivia as a feature. ChatGPT voice mode already handles conversational Q&A. The gap is the gamification layer (scoring, streaks, progression, questions database). This is the most realistic long-term threat.

**Threat 6: Monetization challenge (Internal threat)**
The graveyard of trivia apps (QuizUp, HQ Trivia, Trivia Royale) all failed at monetization despite massive user bases. Hangs's AI evaluation has real per-query costs (OpenAI API). Unlike MCQ apps where evaluation is free (string comparison), every Hangs answer costs money to evaluate. This means Hangs must find paying users earlier than competitors did, or the AI costs will eat the business.

## 6. Actionable Takeaways for MVP Launch

1. **Lead with the differentiator.** "Answer in your own words" is the hook. Every screenshot, App Store listing, and demo should show a free-form voice answer being evaluated by AI -- this is what no one else does.

2. **Do not compete on question volume.** Trivia Crack has millions of questions. Hangs does not need to match that at launch. 200-500 high-quality questions across key categories is sufficient for an MVP. Quality of the *interaction* (voice + AI evaluation + explanation) matters more than quantity.

3. **Target the driving use case first.** "Trivia for your commute" is a concrete, underserved niche. Drivetime validated the market but has not innovated since 2020. CarPlay integration would be a strong moat.

4. **Plan monetization from day one.** Learn from QuizUp and Trivia Royale: do not defer monetization. Options to explore:
   - Freemium: X free AI-evaluated answers per day, subscription for unlimited
   - Tip jar / patronage for solo dev credibility
   - Premium question packs (image questions, specialized topics)

5. **Keep multiplayer for later.** Every defunct competitor was multiplayer-first. Solo voice trivia is the underserved niche. Multiplayer adds complexity, requires critical mass, and is not needed for the core value proposition.

6. **Monitor ChatGPT voice mode.** If OpenAI adds gamification features to voice mode, the competitive landscape changes overnight. Stay aware, ship fast.
