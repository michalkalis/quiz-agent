## 1. TL;DR — highest-leverage, source-backed changes

1. **Answer = punchline.** Make the generator name, in an explicit reasoning step, the wrong assumption the stem installs and how the answer overturns it — no nameable assumption → it's recall, not a reveal ([Dean, joke structure](https://stand-upcomedy.com/glossary/joke-structure/); [Ritchie, forced reinterpretation](https://homepages.abdn.ac.uk/g.ritchie/pages/papers/fr.pdf)). Matches every founder 5/5 (Point Nemo, Dumbledore, Cleopatra).
2. **Retro-obvious test.** Surprise must *resolve*: after the reveal the player says "of course," never "if you say so" ([Suls two-stage model](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Chapter-4-%E2%80%93-A-Two-Stage-Model-for-the-Appreciation-Suls/d1bafaae8d26224d61ae892c8ae600061db7399e)). This is the theoretical name for the founder's "unguessable open answer" defect (spider-silk).
3. **Pinning pass in the reviewer.** Adversarially generate alternative defensible answers, Jeopardy!-style; any unanticipated alternate → revise ([Jeopardy! clue lifecycle](https://www.jeopardy.com/jbuzz/behind-scenes/control-room-lifecycle-jeopardy-clue); [TIME](https://time.com/4936105/jeopardy-final-question-process/)). Fixes the 1904-marathon / cocoa-butter class.
4. **Structured accepted-answer object at generation time** (NAQT `accept`/`reject`/`prompt` model): `{canonical, accept[], reject[], number+tolerance}` authored by the generator, not inferred by the grader ([NAQT correctness guidelines](https://www.naqt.com/rules/correctness-guidelines.html)).
5. **Diversify at generation, select for novelty, rewrite only for craft.** Critique→rewrite improves craft but cannot escape the cliché mode ([Self-Refine](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651); [Barriers to Diversity](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.20408)); inject diversity via Verbalized Sampling ([arXiv:2510.01171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01171)) + deterministic mechanism×topic stratification ([SimpleStrat](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.09038)).
6. **Best-of-N judge needs an explicit novelty term** (embedding distance to corpus + cliché veto) or it re-selects "King of Pop" ([NoveltyBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.05228)).
7. **T/F key-balance gate + convert to MCQ.** 94%-True corpus is the textbook acquiescence exploit ([Haladyna, IDEA Paper #70](https://ideacontent.blob.core.windows.net/content/sites/2/2020/01/IDEA_Paper_70.pdf)); leagues ban YES/NO outright ([Merseyside](https://merseysidequizleagues.org.uk/guidance-to-writers-of-question-sets.aspx)) — the founder's T/F→MCQ ruling is prior-art-backed, not a preference.
8. **Layered leak detection:** lexical clang-association rule ([UWSOM](https://clime.washington.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/WritingMultipleChoiceQuestions.pdf)) → embedding similarity ([SAS](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2108.06130)) → partial-input guessability probe ([Balepur, ACL 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.555/); [BenchMarker](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.06221)).
9. **Answer-line frequency list over the corpus** for cliché detection, quizbowl-style ([QBReader](https://www.qbreader.org/db/); [stock clue](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Stock_clue)) — deterministic, no LLM; the signal is "this *question* is stock," not "this fact is well-known" (mile=1,000 paces is easy AND 5/5).
10. **Voice format rules:** ask-at-the-end operative phrase ([Carberry casebook](https://jeopardy.mattcarberry.com/casebook.html)), first-hearing parseability, 3-option MCQ ([Rodriguez 2005](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1745-3992.2005.00006.x)), numbers graded in normalized numeric space, never string-compared ([NeMo ITN](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2104.05055)).

## 2. Findings by theme

### 2.1 Comedy craft → trivia framing

**Setup→punchline structure.** Dean's model: the setup installs a *target assumption* via an ambiguous connector; the punchline forces a *reinterpretation* that shatters it ([joke structure](https://stand-upcomedy.com/glossary/joke-structure/), [target assumption](https://stand-upcomedy.com/glossary/target-assumption/), [connector](https://stand-upcomedy.com/glossary/connector/)). Ritchie formalizes this as forced reinterpretation: setup has ≥2 readings, punchline forces the hidden one ([Ritchie](https://homepages.abdn.ac.uk/g.ritchie/pages/papers/fr.pdf)). Transfer: a great trivia question is a setup whose *answer* is the reinterpretation — the stem should invite a plausible wrong mental model the answer overturns. The founder's 5/5 "hidden layer" questions (Dumbledore, mile, Lion King) are exactly forced-reinterpretation items.

**Incongruity-resolution — surprise alone is not enough.** Suls (1972): humor requires (1) expectation disconfirmed, then (2) the incongruity *resolved* by a rule that retroactively makes sense of it ([Suls](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Chapter-4-%E2%80%93-A-Two-Stage-Model-for-the-Appreciation-Suls/d1bafaae8d26224d61ae892c8ae600061db7399e); [Ritchie, AISB'99](https://homepages.abdn.ac.uk/g.ritchie/pages/papers/aisb99.pdf)). An answer that stays arbitrary after the reveal (spider-silk → "jumbo jet") fails stage 2.

**Benign violation.** The best facts violate a world-model norm while staying safe — myth-busters, "can't be right… but it is" (ketchup as medicine 4/5, LEGO tyres 5/5) ([McGraw & Warren](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/0956797610376073); [HuRL](https://humorresearchlab.com/benign-violation-theory/)). Generation rule: prefer facts that violate a common *belief* over facts that are merely not widely known.

**Information-gap curiosity.** Curiosity peaks when the player knows enough to sense the gap but not enough to close it; the reveal delivers the pleasure burst ([Loewenstein/Golman, CMU](https://www.cmu.edu/dietrich/sds/docs/golman/golman_loewenstein_curiosity.pdf); [Kidd & Hayden](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4635443/)). This makes guessability the *mechanism* of fun, not a nicety — exactly the founder's "I feel I have a chance to guess" (NZ dancing islands 4/5). Unguessable = no gap tension; leaked = gap pre-closed.

**Economy of setup.** Practitioner consensus: every sentence advances the premise or lands the joke; the funny thing goes last; extra clues dilute misdirection ([Comedy Bizarre](https://comedybizarre.substack.com/p/the-21-most-common-humor-writing); [Vorhaus summary](https://dannybooboo.com/books/comic-toolbox-book-summary-john-vorhaus-review/) — *practitioner/blog sources, low academic authority but convergent with the founder's verbatim "one satisfactory point" ruling*).

**Rule of three / pattern-break.** Two beats establish a pattern, the third subverts it ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rule_of_three_(writing)); [Ken Levine](http://kenlevine.blogspot.com/2017/07/the-comedy-rule-of-3s.html)). Transfers to MCQ distractor design (distractors extend the expected pattern, the key breaks it) and to "which came first" items, which only work when the answer defies the default guess — the founder's Pinocchio-vs-Black-Panther ruling verbatim.

**LLM humor needs decomposed, named comedic operations.** Multistep reasoning (extract associations → find incongruity → craft reveal) beats zero-shot ([Humor Mechanics, arXiv:2405.07280](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.07280)); generic CoT yields "coherent but non-comedic" output ([survey, arXiv:2509.21175](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.21175)).

### 2.2 Trivia-design norms

**Pinning / one unarguable answer.** Jeopardy! researchers actively try to produce alternative correct responses; a clue is "pinned" only when they fail, and every fact is double-sourced ([TIME](https://time.com/4936105/jeopardy-final-question-process/); [Carberry casebook](https://jeopardy.mattcarberry.com/casebook.html)). LearnedLeague's Bushfield drafts with "Are there other ways that you can answer this question?" and distrusts single sources ([Slate](https://slate.com/culture/2021/09/how-to-write-trivia-questions.html)). Quiz leagues require "one unarguable answer" and teach disambiguation templates — "Of which mountain range are the Karakorums a part?" not "Where are the Karakorums?"; pin timeframes for renamed entities ([Merseyside](https://merseysidequizleagues.org.uk/guidance-to-writers-of-question-sets.aspx)).

**Operative phrase last; first-hearing comprehension.** Clues should end with what is being asked; the answer *type* ("this book/author") must be explicit ([Carberry](https://jeopardy.mattcarberry.com/casebook.html); [Berkeley quizbowl primer](https://www.ocf.berkeley.edu/~quizbowl/qb-writing.html)). Questions must be understood in one aural pass ([quiz-league setting guide](https://campnewyork.org/fun/quiz-league-questions/) — *note: hosted on an unrelated-looking domain; treat as a secondary echo of the Merseyside/Airedale norms, not an independent authority*). Critical for voice-while-driving.

**No hoses; uniquely identifying clues.** Quizbowl bans deliberate misdirection that punishes knowledgeable players, and clues that permit multiple answers mid-question ([Berkeley primer](https://www.ocf.berkeley.edu/~quizbowl/qb-writing.html); [QBWiki: Pyramidality](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Pyramidality)). See §4 for the tension with comedy-craft misdirection.

**Format bans.** Leagues: no YES/NO questions, no multi-part answers, one-word answers "usually the most suitable," no trick questions ([Merseyside](https://merseysidequizleagues.org.uk/guidance-to-writers-of-question-sets.aspx)); avoid questions "where only three or four guesses are realistically possible" ([Airedale](https://www.airedalequizleague.net/blog/setting-and-scoring-guidelines)) — T/F is the degenerate case.

**Recognition < recall.** MCQ is systematically easier than free recall because options act as retrieval cues — among the most replicated memory findings ([NN/g](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/recognition-and-recall/); [PsychCentral](https://psychcentral.com/blog/always-learning/2010/01/recognition-vs-recall)). Consequence: converting an unguessable-open item to MCQ is a principled *fairness fix*, exactly the founder's LEGO/Moon ruling.

**Multiple independent inference routes ≠ clue-pile.** Bushfield writes questions with "multiple paths to the right answer" — knowledge, deduction, or inference ([Notre Dame Magazine](https://magazine.nd.edu/stories/not-a-trivial-pursuit/); [Slate](https://slate.com/culture/2021/09/how-to-write-trivia-questions.html)). The transferable rule: an extra clue is allowed only if it opens a *different deduction path*, never a second descriptor of the same referent (the founder's Rome-1/5 clue-pile).

**Gettability.** The answer should be something the player has heard of; the surprise lives in the stem/connection, not an arcane answer; clues shouldn't feel like clues ([QuizQuizQuiz](https://quizquizquiz.com/blog/); [brainpower.events](https://www.brainpower.events/en/the-best-pub-quiz-questions/)).

**Difficulty mix + accessibility floor.** ~30% easy / 50% medium / 20% hard ([quiz-league guide](https://campnewyork.org/fun/quiz-league-questions/)); "don't make your questions too obscure" ([Airedale](https://www.airedalequizleague.net/blog/setting-and-scoring-guidelines)). **Anti-staleness:** don't copy from published quiz books/old sets — recycled material is its own defect class ([Merseyside](https://merseysidequizleagues.org.uk/guidance-to-writers-of-question-sets.aspx)).

### 2.3 LLM creativity elicitation

**Mode collapse is typicality bias from alignment; Verbalized Sampling is the strongest training-free fix.** Prompt the model to verbalize a distribution over k candidates with probabilities, keep the low-typicality tail ([VS, arXiv:2510.01171](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.01171); [site](https://www.verbalized-sampling.com/)). "King of Pop" (founder 2/5) *is* the modal quiz question.

**Temperature alone doesn't escape the mode.** ([SimpleStrat](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.09038)). Min-p sampling makes temp 1.5–2 coherent ([arXiv:2407.01082](https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.01082)) — but only on APIs exposing `min_p` (open models via OpenRouter; **not** Claude/GPT endpoints, so not applicable to the planned Opus generation path).

**Stratify before generating.** Enumerate strata, pick one, generate within it — better coverage at no quality cost ([SimpleStrat](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.09038); [Multi-Novelty](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2502.12700)). Deterministic stratum sampling in code fits the hot-path-LLM-minimalism preference.

**Independent resampling yields near-duplicates.** All frontier models badly lack output diversity; larger models in a family are *less* diverse ([NoveltyBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.05228)). Best-of-N judges must optimize joint novelty×quality or they re-select the mode ([NoveltyBench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.05228); [LLM-as-judge survey](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2411.16594)).

**QDAIF corpus steering.** Archive binned by qualitative dimensions; keep candidates that fill empty niches or beat incumbents ([QDAIF, arXiv:2310.13032](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.13032)). Maps onto the existing pattern-quota machinery.

**Persona rotation beats one "creative" persona.** Many distinct ordinary personas outperform a single creative-genius persona; effects real but modest and task-dependent ([arXiv:2511.07448](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.07448); [arXiv:2602.20408](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.20408); [LLM Discussion](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.06373); [persona evaluation](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2507.16076)).

**Surprise scaffolds: force the leap as an explicit stage.** CLoT/CLoST: first pick a remote association, then connect back ([CLoT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.02439); [CLoST](https://arxiv.org/abs/2410.10370)); surprise correlates with high-perplexity content ([Unfunny LLMs, ACL 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-short.76.pdf)). Converges with Humor Mechanics (§2.1) and retro-justifies the `v2_cot` wave being the founder's peak (4.50).

**Critique→rewrite improves craft, not novelty.** Self-Refine's gains are real ([arXiv:2303.17651](https://arxiv.org/abs/2303.17651)) but instruction-level creativity cues stay inside the RLHF attractor basin ([Barriers to Diversity, arXiv:2602.20408](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.20408)). Diversity must be injected at sampling/seeding time; keep the rewrite pass exclusively for the craft-defect checklist.

**Cliché suppression via curated pattern lists works at scale.** ([Antislop, arXiv:2510.15061](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2510.15061); [EQ-Bench](https://eqbench.com/about.html)). Validates a curated cliché-question list as a hard reviewer check.

*(Provenance note: several load-bearing citations here are very recent arXiv preprints — VS 2510, Antislop 2510/ICLR'26, Barriers 2602, BenchMarker 2602 — not yet peer-review-hardened. Directionally consistent with the older peer-reviewed base (QDAIF ICLR'24, CLoT CVPR'24, min-p ICLR'25), so safe to act on, but tag the specific numbers as preprint-grade.)*

### 2.4 Answer design & auto-grading (voice)

**NAQT answer lines — author the acceptance set at generation time.** Canonical answer + `or`/`accept`/`prompt on`/`do not accept` directives, required-vs-optional name parts marked ([NAQT correctness guidelines](https://www.naqt.com/rules/correctness-guidelines.html); [QBWiki: Prompt](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Prompt); applied to automated judging in [NAQT Buzzword](https://www.naqt.com/buzzword/judging.jsp)). Decades-tested prior art for machine-checkable answers; the generator (which knows the fact) decides "is 'driving' enough?" — not the grader at runtime.

**Distractor craft is codified.** Haladyna/Downing/Rodriguez: every distractor plausible (built from real misconceptions), options homogeneous in content/grammar/length, exactly one defensible key, no "all/none of the above," don't cue the key by length ([guidelines review](https://site.ufvjm.edu.br/fammuc/files/2016/05/item-writing-guidelines.pdf); [ExamEval](https://www.exameval.com/articles/flaws/heterogeneous-answer-choices); [CMU digest](https://metals.hcii.cmu.edu/wp-content/uploads/2019/12/How-to-Write-a-Multiple-Choice-Question-According-to-the-Research.pdf)).

**Three options are optimal.** 80-year meta-analysis: 3-option MCQs match 4–5-option psychometrically; extra distractors are non-functional ([Rodriguez 2005](https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1745-3992.2005.00006.x); [summary](https://tipsforteachers.co.uk/research-three-options-are-optimal-for-multiple-choice-items/)). Doubly decisive for voice: options cost listening time and driver working memory.

**LLM distractor generation is benchmarked.** EMNLP 2024 survey of 60 studies ([ACL Anthology](https://aclanthology.org/2024.emnlp-main.799/)); plausibility measured by model-uncertainty induced, not text overlap ([D-GEN](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.13439); [ACL 2025](https://aclanthology.org/2025.acl-long.1154.pdf); [math distractors](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.02124)).

**Numbers: grade in normalized numeric space.** "Twenty twenty" is ambiguous in ASR output; inverse text normalization is the production standard ([NeMo ITN](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2104.05055); [Gladia](https://www.gladia.io/blog/text-normalization-speech-recognition)). Never string-compare numbers; parse both sides, compare with tolerance/units.

**Layered fuzzy+phonetic matching.** Exact-after-normalization → Double Metaphone → edit distance; one documented stack hit 96% on names, and phonetic-alone over-accepts ([ML6](https://www.ml6.eu/en/blog/why-voice-ai-fails-at-name-matching-and-how-we-achieved-96-accuracy); [Double Metaphone](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/234803025_The_Double_Metaphone_Search_Algorithm)). Quiz apps mirror it: length-scaled typo tolerance, exact match required for numbers ([TriviaMaker](https://triviamaker.com/allow-typos-in-quiz-answers)); alternative accepted answers ([Kahoot](https://support.kahoot.com/hc/en-us/articles/115002308428-Kahoot-question-types)).

**LLM-as-grader = fallback tier only.** GPT-4-class rubric graders match fine-tuned BERT ([LAK 2025](https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3706468.3706481); [ASAG2024](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2409.18596); [RAG-ASAG](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.05276v1)) but are slow/costly — deterministic tiers first.

**Voice platforms force selected-response.** Alexa's own trivia samples match on option content or ordinal ("answer one"), an implicit ruling that open speech grading is only safe with a constrained answer space ([buttons-trivia](https://github.com/alexa-samples/skill-sample-nodejs-buttons-trivia); [quiz-game](https://github.com/alexa-samples/skill-sample-nodejs-quiz-game)).

### 2.5 Anti-pattern detection heuristics

**T/F key balance.** Balancing the key is a canonical item-writing rule ([Haladyna, IDEA #70](https://ideacontent.blob.core.windows.net/content/sites/2/2020/01/IDEA_Paper_70.pdf); [2002 rules summary](https://cpl.health.unm.edu/AssetListing/Writing-Questions-for-Learning-and-Assessment-JiTL-2573/Rules-for-Multiple-Choice-Items-Haladyna-et-al-2002-5943)); acquiescence bias makes "guess true" a winning strategy on unbalanced keys ([IJ STEM Education](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40594-019-0169-0); [MTF review](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1747938X21000324)); some guides recommend slightly *more false* items since false discriminates better ([U. Minnesota](https://survey.umn.edu/exams/additional-resources/how-to-write-test-questions); [itemwriting.co](https://itemwriting.co/true-false/)). **Length is a secondary tell:** true statements run longer (padded with qualifiers to be defensibly true) ([itemwriting.co](https://itemwriting.co/true-false/)) — the formal name for the founder's "telegraphed T/F." Absolute terms (always/never) cue false ([UWSOM](https://clime.washington.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/WritingMultipleChoiceQuestions.pdf)).

**Answer-leak / cluing.** "Clang association" — key echoes stem wording — is a named classic flaw ([UWSOM](https://clime.washington.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/07/WritingMultipleChoiceQuestions.pdf); [Pitfalls, PMC](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3045096/)); audits find cue flaws in ~10–12.5% of exam items ([PMC](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC5041405/)). **Partial-input baselines** are the proven ML detection: hypothesis-only NLI baselines beat majority class ([Poliak 2018](https://arxiv.org/abs/1805.01042); [Gururangan 2018](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/325449053_Annotation_Artifacts_in_Natural_Language_Inference_Data)); choices-only prompting beat majority in 11/12 LLM×dataset combos ([Balepur, ACL 2024](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.555/)). **BenchMarker** is the closest reference implementation: rule checks + LLM judges + partial-input baselines over a flaw taxonomy incl. guessability and answer-position bias, human-validated ([arXiv:2602.06221](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.06221)). Off-the-shelf overlap metrics: QCLO lexical ratio ([arXiv:2109.11256](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2109.11256)), SAS embedding similarity ([arXiv:2108.06130](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2108.06130)).

**Over-exposure / cliché.** Quiz communities name these "chestnuts"/"stock clues" ([QBWiki](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Stock_clue)) and detect them empirically with answer-line frequency lists over 300k+ question DBs ([QBReader](https://www.qbreader.org/db/); [question databases](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Question_databases)); paraphrase repeats need semantic matching ([hsquizbowl forum](https://hsquizbowl.org/forums/viewtopic.php?t=28339)). LLM contamination methods transfer as a familiarity probe: n-gram overlap, perplexity probes, guided completion ([contamination taxonomy](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.08716); [arXiv:2402.15938](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2402.15938); [LAMA](https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.01066)). **Founder-data caveat:** fact familiarity ≠ cliché (mile=1,000 paces is easy yet 5/5) — weight verbatim-phrasing frequency over raw fact-perplexity.

## 4. Contradictions & open calls

**4.1 Comedy misdirection vs quizbowl's hose ban.** Dean/Ritchie *prescribe* installing a wrong assumption ([Dean](https://stand-upcomedy.com/glossary/target-assumption/)); quizbowl *bans* misdirection that punishes knowledgeable players ([Berkeley](https://www.ocf.berkeley.edu/~quizbowl/qb-writing.html)). Not actually incompatible once scoped: the wrong assumption must be a *folk belief the answer corrects* (benign violation), never a trap that makes a knowledgeable player answer wrong. Founder's 5/5s are all belief-corrections, not traps. **Call: adopt comedy misdirection, add a no-hose reviewer check as the guardrail.**

**4.2 LearnedLeague clue-stacking vs founder's one-hook rule.** Bushfield stacks multiple clues ([Slate](https://slate.com/culture/2021/09/how-to-write-trivia-questions.html)); founder rated Rome's clue-pile 1/5. Resolved, don't average: LL stacks *independent inference routes*; Rome stacked *redundant descriptors*. Rule = "at most one extra clue, and only if it opens a different deduction path." Founder ruling remains the default (one hook); the LL exception is narrow.

**4.3 T/F fix: balance keys vs eliminate the format.** Psychometrics says balance ~50/50, some sources say slightly more false ([U. Minnesota](https://survey.umn.edu/exams/additional-resources/how-to-write-test-questions)); leagues ban YES/NO outright ([Merseyside](https://merseysidequizleagues.org.uk/guidance-to-writers-of-question-sets.aspx)); founder ruled T/F→MCQ. **Founder + leagues win: convert/transform is primary; the 40–60% balance gate is the backstop for any T/F that survives.** Sub-tension: founder's T/F→MCQ implies 2 options; Rodriguez says 3 optimal. For converted T/F, the founder's own transform rule (extract the surprising number, ask it as 3-option MCQ — Golf Q36) is the better path than a 2-option render. Flag the 2-vs-3 render choice as an open product call.

**4.4 Prescriptive scaffolds vs Anthropic's "give general instructions."** Humor Mechanics/CLoT show named multistep comedic operations beat freeform ([arXiv:2405.07280](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.07280); [CLoT](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.02439)); Anthropic advises general instructions for creative tasks — "Claude's creativity may exceed your ability to prescribe the optimal process" ([Claude best practices](https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/build-with-claude/prompt-engineering/claude-prompting-best-practices)). Empirical tie-breaker exists in our own data: the scaffolded `v2_cot` wave is the founder's peak (4.50). **Call: scaffold the *stages* (find assumption → design misdirection → verify resolution), leave the *content* of each stage free. Validate in Phase 4.**

**4.5 Guessability probe vs deducible-by-design.** Partial-input probes flag questions a model answers without full input ([Balepur](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.555/)) — but the founder *loves* deducible questions (umbrella→UK 4.5/5, Hogwarts motto Q28 "possible to deduct the answer"). The probe must separate leak (rationale cites a stem phrase) from deduction (rationale reasons); BenchMarker models this LLM-judge distinction ([arXiv:2602.06221](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2602.06221)). **Implementation risk to watch in Phase 2 validation against the 36 rated questions.**

**4.6 Familiarity ≠ cliché.** Perplexity/contamination probes score *fact* familiarity; founder data shows easy-familiar facts can be 5/5 (mile Q13) while the defect is *stock questions* (King of Pop). Weight corpus/verbatim-phrasing frequency and the "stock question" judge over raw perplexity ([QBWiki stock clue](https://www.qbwiki.com/wiki/Stock_clue) supports the question-level framing). Sources don't disagree here so much as measure different things — don't launder one into the other.

**4.7 Source-strength flags (not laundered).**
- Comedy "economy of setup" rests on practitioner blogs (Comedy Bizarre substack, Vorhaus book-summary blog) — convergent with the founder ruling and Dean's theory, but not academic evidence.
- The [campnewyork.org quiz-league guide](https://campnewyork.org/fun/quiz-league-questions/) (30/50/20 mix, first-hearing rule) is an oddly-hosted secondary source; the Merseyside/Airedale primary guides carry the same norms — cite those where possible.
- 2025–2026 arXiv preprints (VS, Antislop, Barriers-to-Diversity, BenchMarker, CLoST) are load-bearing but not yet peer-review-hardened; directionally corroborated by peer-reviewed QDAIF/CLoT/min-p. Treat specific effect sizes as preprint-grade.
- The min-p recommendation is **inapplicable** to the planned Claude-Opus generation path (parameter not exposed); it only matters if generation ever routes to open models via OpenRouter.

**4.8 Open product calls for the founder (echoing the plan's list, now research-informed).**
- 2-option vs 3-option render for converted T/F (4.3 above).
- Cliché policy: hard reject vs freshness-reframe — Antislop suppresses-and-regenerates rather than discards, which supports "reframe" as the cheaper default.
- How aggressive the guessability probe should be before Phase 2 validation proves it doesn't kill deducible-by-design questions.
