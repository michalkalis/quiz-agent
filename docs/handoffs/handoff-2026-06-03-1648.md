## Decisions

- **Run ralph on the laptop, not mba.** mba's `agent` user is non-admin (`staff` only, no sudo). The Xcode 26.3 license accept writes to `/Library/Preferences/com.apple.dt.Xcode.plist` (root:wheel) — there is no per-user override. Without the license, `xcodebuild`, `xcrun`, and `simctl` all refuse. The no-admin workaround (expand xip into `~/Applications`, select via `DEVELOPER_DIR`) gets us to `xcodebuild -version` but not past the license gate. The one-command fix (`sudo DEVELOPER_DIR=… xcodebuild -license accept`) requires admin access mba doesn't grant to `agent`. Laptop builds on iOS 26 / iPhone 17 Pro without any of this — it's the clean host.
- **Ralph/human split by verifiability, not "is it iOS"** (D5, recorded in issue-45). 45.1–45.6 are pure Swift logic/components with unit/inspector/`.dump` acceptance — ralph-suitable. 45.7–45.13 are integration/visual/simulator — human.
- **issue-45 supersedes #42 Track E (42.14–42.18).** Both cover the same MCQ-voice iOS work. issue-45 adds the design-port scope and uses the richer #42 Track E detail (`MCQTranscriptMatcher`, SK ordinals, `AnswerConfirmationView` reuse). Doing the work in #42 would leave the design-port orphaned; issue-45 is the single place.
- **D4 (reveal-on-result UX) is deferred to human track 45.7.** Ralph builds the 4-state `AnswerOption` capability; wiring it into the result flow is a product decision (in-place reveal vs. keep `ResultView`). Ralph must not guess it.
- **`launch-issue45.sh` is for mba only.** On the laptop, call `ralph.sh` directly with the laptop's simulator destination. The launcher has mba-specific DEVELOPER_DIR + iPhone 16 Pro target baked in.
