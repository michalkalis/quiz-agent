This relationship is part of Kleiber's Law and the broader metabolic theory of ecology. The mathematics: a mouse lives about 3 years at ~500 beats per minute = 500 × 60 × 24 × 365 × 3 ≈ 790 million beats. In a real sense, modern medicine has given humans extra heartbeats beyond what evolution "budgeted" for our body size.

**Q:** Identical twins share the same DNA — they are, genetically, the same person split into two bodies. Yet forensic scientists have been able to tell identical twins apart for over a century using a method based on the assumption that no two people are alike. **A:** Fingerprints are not directly encoded by DNA. While genes influence the general characteristics of fingerprint formation — the overall pattern type (loop, whorl, or arch), the ridge density, and the approximate size of the print — the specific micro-details that uniquely identify a fingerprint (ridge endings, bifurcations, and the positions of individual minutiae points) are determined by random physical forces during fetal development, not by genetic sequence. Fingerprints form between about weeks 10 and 24 of gestation, as friction ridges develop on the fingertip skin. The precise shape they take depends on chaotic physical processes: the rate of skin growth at each fingertip, the pressure from adjacent fingers in the confined space of the womb, the exact position of the fetus at each moment of ridge formation, local differences in amniotic fluid pressure and flow, and random variation in gene expression between cells that carry the same DNA. Two fetuses with identical genomes will be in slightly different positions in the uterus, experiencing slightly different mechanical forces, and their ridge patterns will diverge. The technical term is "epigenetic difference due to developmental stochasticity." The principle extends beyond twins: a cloned animal would not share its progenitor's fingerprints, because cloning copies the genome, not the developmental environment. Identical twins themselves are typically aware that their fingerprints differ, and forensic databases confirm this.
