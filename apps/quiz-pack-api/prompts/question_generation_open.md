## What "open-shape" means

An open-shape question asks for a **mechanism, cause, or puzzle resolution** —
the player reasons toward the answer rather than recalling a single fact. Two
sub-kinds:

1. **Open mechanism ("why/how")** — e.g. *"Why are Ferraris traditionally red?"*
   The answer is web-verifiable (national racing colours), but it is naturally a
   short explanation, not a one-word name.
2. **Lateral-thinking puzzle** — a self-contained situation with a surprising but
   logical resolution that **no web source states directly**; it must be
   deducible from the setup alone.

If the answer you have in mind is a single name, number, year, or winner, this
is a **closed** question — do NOT generate it here. Stop and skip it.

---

## The two-field answer contract (READ CAREFULLY)

Every open question MUST return BOTH:

- **`headline_answer`** — the short, gettable gist a player would say aloud,
  **≤ 8 words**. This is what the evaluator scores. It is the "did they get it?"
  core, stripped of justification. Example: *"It was the Italian national racing
  colour."* → headline `"Italian racing colour"`.
- **`explanation`** — the full context read aloud AFTER the reveal: the
  reasoning, the surprise, the detail. Sentences are fine here. This is the ONLY
  place verbose prose belongs.

Also populate:

- **`alternative_answers`** — every other phrasing or distinct resolution a fair
  player could give that must be accepted as correct. For puzzles with more than
  one valid reading, list each. Never leave this empty for an open question.

**Sentence-answer exception:** `headline_answer` may be a short phrase or clause
(≤ 8 words), unlike the factual branch's ≤5-word canonical names. It must still
be punchy enough to speak hands-free while driving. The full sentence lives in
`explanation`, never in `headline_answer`.

For lateral puzzles with no web source, set `source_url` to null — the open
branch is exempt from the hard source requirement (verified instead by logical
consistency).

---

## Quality bar

- The setup must be **self-contained** — a fair player can deduce the answer
  from the question alone, with no outside lookup.
- The resolution must **uniquely follow** from the setup (or every valid
  alternative is listed in `alternative_answers`).
- Universal appeal: works across cultures, no English-only wordplay.
- `question` text: 1–3 sentences. Read it aloud; if it takes over 12 seconds,
  trim it.
