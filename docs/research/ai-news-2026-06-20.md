## 1. OpenRouter compound routing — `openrouter:advisor` and `openrouter:subagent` tools (June 11)

**Sources:**
- [OpenRouter June 2026 roundup — DigitalApplied](https://www.digitalapplied.com/blog/openrouter-new-models-june-2026-roundup-pricing-rankings) *(primary openrouter.ai blog blocked 403 this run — treat as unverified until confirmed)*

**Summary:** On June 11, OpenRouter announced two server-side compound routing tools distinct from Fusion. `openrouter:advisor` lets a fast/cheap model (e.g. gpt-4o-mini) consult a stronger model mid-generation when it decides the request warrants escalation; the strong model's response is folded back in. `openrouter:subagent` goes the other direction: a frontier model can hand off a self-contained sub-task (summarise a doc, extract structured data) to a smaller, cheaper worker without burning frontier tokens on that subtask. These are in-generation routing primitives, not a fan-out pattern like Fusion.

**Relevance to us:** Directly relevant to two existing pipelines:
1. Our best-of-N question generator in `apps/quiz-pack-api` currently always calls gpt-4o for generation and a separate critique call. `openrouter:advisor` could replace the critique escalation — generate with gpt-4o-mini, escalate only questions that fail the quality threshold.
2. Our A/B multi-model scoring experiment (claude-sonnet-4-6 + gpt-4.1-mini) maps to the advisor pattern: run gpt-4.1-mini first, call claude-sonnet-4-6 only when the mini model signals low confidence.

**Suggested action:** Verify the announcement via [openrouter.ai/blog/announcements](https://openrouter.ai/blog/announcements) directly (blocked during this run). If confirmed, open an issue to evaluate `openrouter:advisor` as a cost-reduction layer in the best-of-N pipeline.

**Watch or act:** Watch until primary source is confirmed; then actionable.
