## Recap

Short diagnostic session. User reported TTS (question read-aloud) being significantly quieter than background music at the same system volume. Full root-cause analysis was performed: the iOS app holds the `AVAudioSession` in `.playAndRecord` category for the entire quiz duration, which applies a ~6–10 dB system-level playback attenuation compared to the `.playback` category used by music apps. Compounded by `.mixWithOthers` when a music app is active simultaneously. No code was changed yet — a research artifact and written fix plan were produced.

## Decisions

- **Root cause is the category, not the player volume.** `AVPlayer.volume` is already 1.0; the gain deficit is from iOS's system-level attenuation on `.playAndRecord`. Confirmed by Apple devforum thread 721535 (unresolved Apple bug across iOS 13–18).
- **Category switch is the right fix, not `overrideOutputAudioPort(.speaker)`.** The override would force built-in speaker and break Bluetooth in-car playback — the primary use case. Rejected.
- **Silence detection stop/restart window makes the switch safe.** `QuizViewModel+Audio.swift:85` already stops the `AVAudioEngine` before TTS; no risk of engine conflict during the `.playback` window.
- **`.duckOthers` preferred over `.mixWithOthers` during TTS.** Makes a background music app duck instead of compete. Better perceptual contrast for driving.
